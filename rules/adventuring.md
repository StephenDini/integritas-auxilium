# Adventuring Reference

## Time Units

| Unit | Duration | Used For |
|------|----------|---------|
| Round | 10 seconds | Combat, encounters |
| Turn | 10 minutes (60 rounds) | Dungeon exploration |
| Hour | 6 turns | Resources |
| Day | — | Wilderness travel, rest |

## Encumbrance

### Basic Encumbrance (Optional)
Tracks treasure weight only. Equipment does not count.

| Armour Worn | Without Treasure | Carrying Treasure |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Unarmoured | 120' (40') | 90' (30') |
| Light armour | 90' (30') | 60' (20') |
| Heavy armour | 60' (20') | 30' (10') |

### Detailed Encumbrance (Optional)
Tracks treasure + armour + weapons. Misc. gear = 80 coins.

| Total Weight | Movement |
|-------------|----------|
| Up to 400 cn | 120' (40') |
| Up to 600 cn | 90' (30') |
| Up to 800 cn | 60' (20') |
| Up to 1,600 cn | 30' (10') |
| Over 1,600 cn | Cannot move |

**Common item weights (coins):** Coin (any) 1 cn, Gem 1 cn, Jewellery 10 cn, Scroll 1 cn, Wand 10 cn, Potion 10 cn, Rod 20 cn, Staff 40 cn

See equipment.md for weapon/armour weights.

## Ability Checks

Roll 1d20. Result ≤ ability score = success. Modifiers: –4 (easy) to +4 (very difficult). Natural 1 = always success; natural 20 = always failure.

---

## Damage, Healing, and Death

- **Death**: Reduced to 0 HP or less.
- **Natural healing**: 1d3 HP per full day of complete rest (rest interrupted = no healing that day).
- **Magical healing**: Instantaneous; combines with natural healing.
- **Falling**: 1d6 per 10' fallen onto hard surface.

### Destroyed Equipment
If killed by destructive spell/breath: equipment assumed destroyed. Magic items may get a saving throw using character's save values (+bonus of the item).

## Ability Scores — Quick Reference

| Score | STR | INT | WIS | DEX | CON | CHA |
|-------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 3 | –3 | –3 | –3 | –3 | –3 | –3 |
| 4–5 | –2 | –2 | –2 | –2 | –2 | –2 |
| 6–8 | –1 | –1 | –1 | –1 | –1 | –1 |
| 9–12 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| 13–15 | +1 | +1 | +1 | +1 | +1 | +1 |
| 16–17 | +2 | +2 | +2 | +2 | +2 | +2 |
| 18 | +3 | +3 | +3 | +3 | +3 | +3 |

**STR**: Open stuck doors (d6, add bonus); bonus to hit/damage in melee.
**INT**: Bonus languages (1 per +1 over 0); affects MU/Elf spell-learning.
**WIS**: Saving throw bonus vs magic (same as modifier above).
**DEX**: Bonus to missile to-hit; modifier to AC (optional); initiative modifier (optional individual initiative).
**CON**: Modifier to HP per die (min 1/die; stops at 9 HD). Does not apply above 9 HD.
**CHA**: NPC reaction bonus; retainer morale modifier.

**Open Doors (STR):** Roll 1d6; need 5+ normally, modifier adjusts target (e.g., STR 16–17 = +2 = opens on 3+).

## Dungeon Adventuring

### Sequence of Play (Per Turn)
1. **Wandering monsters**: Check every 2 turns (1-in-6 chance).
2. **Actions**: Party decides movement, searching, etc.
3. **Description**: Referee describes results; resolve encounters if any.
4. **End of turn**: Update light source durations, spell durations, rest tracking.

### Movement in Dungeon
- **Exploring (unknown)**: Move base movement rate in feet per turn (very slow; mapping + caution).
- **Familiar areas**: Up to 3× base movement rate per turn (referee discretion).
- **In encounters**: Move ⅓ base movement rate per round.
- **Running (pursuit)**: Move full base movement rate per round. Exhausted after 30 rounds (–2 attack, damage, AC; lasts until 3 full turns of rest).

### Resting in Dungeon
Rest 1 turn per hour. Skipping rest: –1 to attack and damage rolls.

### Doors
- **Secret doors**: Search 10'×10' area = 1-in-6 chance (elves/thieves may have better odds).
- **Stuck doors**: Force open by STR roll. Failed attempt = eliminate any surprise vs monsters behind door.
- **Locked doors**: Requires lock picks (thief class), or magic.
- **Listening**: 1-in-6 chance (elves 2-in-6). Referee rolls secretly. One attempt per door, per character. Undead make no noise.
- **Swinging shut**: Doors tend to close; wedge with iron spikes to hold open.

### Traps
- **Trigger chance**: 2-in-6 each time action could trigger trap.
- **Finding room traps**: Search 10'×10' = 1-in-6 chance. Takes 1 turn.
- **Finding treasure traps**: Thief class ability (see thief.md for %).
- **Damage**: Usually automatic, no attack roll.

### Searching
All searching takes 1 turn. Referee rolls secretly. One attempt per character per area/item.

### Wandering Monsters (Dungeon)
- Check every 2 turns.
- 1-in-6 chance of encounter.
- Distance: 2d6×10 feet away, moving toward party.
- Noise/light increases chance; hiding quietly decreases it.

## Wilderness Adventuring

### Overland Travel
Miles per day = base movement rate ÷ 5.

| Terrain | Speed Modifier |
|---------|---------------|
| Clear, grasslands, roads | Normal (roads = +50%) |
| Broken, desert, forest, hills | –33% |
| Jungle, mountains, swamp | –50% |

**Maintained roads**: +50% speed.

**Forced march**: +50% distance for 1 day; must rest full day afterward.

**Resting**: 1 full day off per 6 days of travel. Skipping: –1 to hit and damage.

### Flying
Miles per day = 2× overland rate. Terrain doesn't affect air travel.

### Flying Mounts — Carrying Capacity
- 3 HD: carry half-sized being
- 6 HD: carry adult human
- 12 HD: carry horse
- 24 HD: carry elephant

### Losing Direction (Wilderness)
Roll at start of each travel day:
- Clear, grasslands: 1-in-6
- Barren, hills, mountains, woods: 2-in-6
- Desert, jungle, swamp: 3-in-6

Following trails/roads/guides: no chance of getting lost.

### Foraging and Hunting
- **Foraging**: During normal travel. 1-in-6 chance/day to find food for 1d6 humans.
- **Hunting**: Full day activity (no travel). 1-in-6 chance of finding suitable animals (plus normal wandering monster check).

### Visibility
Open terrain: see 3 miles. May be reduced (forest) or increased (hilltop).

### Wandering Monsters (Wilderness)
- Typically 1 check/day (up to 3–4).
- Chance by terrain:
  - City, clear, grassland, settled: 1-in-6
  - Aerial, barren, desert, forest, hills: 2-in-6
  - Jungle, mountains, swamp: 3-in-6
- Distance: 4d6×10 yards (surprised: 1d4×10 yards).

### Wilderness Sequence (Per Day)
1. Decide course.
2. Referee checks for lost direction.
3. Wandering monster check(s).
4. Referee describes terrain/encounters.
5. End of day: update rations, spell durations, rest.

## Waterborne Adventuring

Water movement: miles/day = MV ÷ 5. (Same formula as overland.)

**River travel**: Current may add or subtract 1d6+6 miles per day.

**Losing Direction**: With navigator: 2-in-6. Without navigator: 100% on open seas, 2-in-6 in sight of land.

**Wandering monsters**: 1 check/day (up to 3–4). Chance: 2-in-6 on oceans/rivers, 3-in-6 in swamps.

**Encounter distance**: 4d6×10 yards (surprised: 1d4×10 yards).

**Visibility**: Land visible 24 miles. Ships visible 300 yards (clear) or 40 yards (dense fog).

### Wind Conditions (Roll 2d6 at start of each day)
| 2d6 | Wind | Effect |
|-----|------|--------|
| 2 | No wind | Sailing impossible; oars at ⅓ speed |
| 3 | Faint breeze | Sail at ⅓ speed |
| 4 | Gentle breeze | Sail at ½ speed |
| 5 | Moderate breeze | Sail at ⅔ speed |
| 6–8 | Fresh breeze | Normal sail speed |
| 9 | Strong breeze | Sail speed +⅓ |
| 10 | High wind | Sail speed +½ |
| 11 | Near gale | Sail speed ×2; seaworthy 10% take-on-water, unseaworthy 20% |
| 12 | Gale | Sail speed ×3 in random direction; seaworthy: 75% wreck if hitting land; unseaworthy: 80% sink |

## Encounters

### Surprise
- Roll 1d6 per side (sides not already aware).
- 1–2 = surprised (cannot act that round).
- Light in darkness usually prevents surprising opponents.
- If surrounded by 3+ monsters: monsters may encircle.

### Encounter Sequence
1. Surprise check (if applicable).
2. Determine encounter distance.
3. Initiative (both non-surprised sides).
4. Actions resolved.
5. One turn has passed.

### Monster Reaction (2d6 + CHA modifier)
| 2d6 | Reaction |
|-----|---------|
| 2 or less | Hostile, attacks |
| 3–5 | Unfriendly, may attack |
| 6–8 | Neutral, uncertain |
| 9–11 | Indifferent, uninterested |
| 12+ | Friendly, helpful |

### Encounter Distance
- Dungeon: 2d6×10 feet
- Wilderness: 4d6×10 yards (surprised: 1d4×10 yards)
- Waterborne: 4d6×10 yards (surprised: 1d4×10 yards)

### Evasion and Pursuit

**Dungeon**: If fleeing side is faster, evasion automatic. If not faster, pursuit occurs. Dropping treasure or food = 3-in-6 chance pursuer stops.

**Wilderness**: Percentile roll vs table below. Neither side surprised = use group size table.

| Fleeing Group Size | 1 pursuer | 2–3 pursuers | 4+ pursuers |
|--------------------|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| 1–4 | 50% | 70% | 90% |
| 5–12 | 35% | 50% | 70% |
| 13–24 | 25% | 35% | 50% |
| 25+ | 10% | 25% | 35% |

Surprised side: generally cannot evade. Surprising side: automatically evades.
Modifiers: If one side is twice the speed, adjust ±25%. Dense terrain may +25%.
Minimum escape chance: 5%.

**Waterborne Evasion**:
| Speed Compared to Pursuer | Chance |
|---------------------------|--------|
| Faster | 80% |
| 0–30' slower | 50% |
| 31–60' slower | 40% |
| 61–90' slower | 35% |
| 91–120' slower | 25% |
| 121'+ slower | 10% |

## NPC Retainers and Hirelings

**Maximum retainers**: Based on CHA:

| CHA | Max Retainers | Retainer Morale |
|-----|--------------|----------------|
| 3 | 1 | 4 |
| 4–5 | 2 | 5 |
| 6–8 | 3 | 6 |
| 9–12 | 4 | 7 |
| 13–15 | 5 | 8 |
| 16–17 | 6 | 9 |
| 18 | 7 | 10 |

**Retainer morale**: Check when in danger, suffer significant losses, or ordered to do something very dangerous. Roll 2d6; meet or beat morale score = holds. Failure = flees or quits.

**Pay**: Retainers typically receive a half share of treasure and XP.

## Strongholds

- **Fighter**: May build castle at any level with sufficient funds. At 9th level: granted noble title (Baron/Baroness); controls surrounding lands as a Barony.
- **Cleric**: At 8th level: may establish a place of worship; receive monthly donations from faithful.
- **Magic-User**: At 11th level: may build stronghold; 1d6 apprentice magic-users of levels 1–3 arrive to study.
- **Thief**: At 9th level: may establish a thieves' guild; receive income from 2d6 1st-level thieves.
- **Elf**: At 9th level: may build stronghold in forest; forest animals within 5 miles become friendly and warn of intruders; may only hire elven mercenaries (but can have retainers of any race).

## Swimming

Movement rate: Half normal. Everyone can swim unless there's a clear reason they cannot.

Drowning risk is at referee's discretion based on conditions (rough water, heavy armour, fighting in water, etc.).
