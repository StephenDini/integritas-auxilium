### Three Minotaurs — Room 72
Behind a barred door (bull silhouette). Party scouted via drilled peephole + crystal ball, got a **surprise round**. Bruce's **Lightning Bolt** (chained: 20/10/10, then a second bolt for 22) killed all three; **Hold Person failed** (minotaurs are monstrosities, not humanoid). **825 XP** (275 each). Loot: **4 gems worth 1,700 gp**.

### Hell Hounds (Copper-Pile Room, below the trap door)
Two ladders descend from a "natural death" painting room into a chamber holding **~9,000–10,000 copper pieces (~90 gp)** guarded by fire-breathing **hell hounds**. The intended bypass: **toss a single copper coin into the pile** and the trained hounds let you pass — the party fought instead. Burt tried feeding them from his bag of holding (beef jerky, the scorpion corpse, wine, the lion's-head trophy), thinking they might be friendly; they breathed fire (14 dmg to Burt). Killed with Rowan's flaming sword, arrows, lightning, and magic missile. NPC looters ("three guys on bicycles") fled with copper. **1,007 XP.**

Also in the room (the DM initially forgot to mention it): a **Wand of Trap Detection — 12 charges** (star-tipped), taken by Burt.

**Potion mishap:** during the fight, Burt drank one of several unidentified potions he'd carried since the desert and was turned to **gaseous form** for ~10–20 minutes (harmless but useless — he floated, couldn't fight, and reappeared "naked" later). As **rat-folk**, Burt can normally identify a potion by scent, but he chugged it instead of sniffing.

### Wooden Statue-Creatures — Room 66
Small (~3-ft, solid wood, ~500 lb) man-like animate statues in a 50×50 hexagonal room. Bruce cast **Web** (no save) to immobilize them, and the party destroyed them with ranged attacks (spears, daggers, mace, magic missile) as they hung helpless (no XP was announced). A **trap door** in the floor (spotted by Fraxiga) led down to the hell-hound/copper level; **Fraxiga cast Spider Climb** and carried the blink dog down the ladder (the dog couldn't climb).

### Two Medusas — Room 69 ("Abandoned Slave Barracks")
The barred door bore a **serpent silhouette**; Bruce's crystal ball scry showed two women (glitchy/comedic reception). **Petrification** threat. **Burt was turned to stone** (he wore a minotaur head to look through its mouth-hole but still failed his save). The party fought looking at the floor / blind (–4 to hit) and killed both. **350 XP** (175 each). Burt was restored with Dougal's **Stone to Flesh** scroll. Loot: **11 gems (~1,100 gp)**, a second **rope of climbing**, a **potion of heroism** (lets the drinker fight as one level higher — HP, saves, abilities — temporarily; kept for later), an arcane scroll, and a **ring of strength (+1 STR)** (given to a party member, bumping STR 15 → 16).

### Displacer Beasts — Room 77 (Blink Dog Death)

The dog's remains were bagged in case of future raising. (Wand of Wonder now ~96 charges.)

## Discoveries / Rules Notes

- **Wand of Wonder mechanic (confirmed):** roll d100 and spend a charge; the **first roll is a delusion** (what the wielder *believes* happens), the **second roll is the real effect**. This use produced a real Fireball (which killed the blink dog). Held by **Bruce**; ~98 charges when acquired, so it is being drawn down. See `items/wand-of-wonder.md`.
- **Paralysis antidote vs fear-paralysis:** DM ruled a paralysis antidote does **not** cure fear-induced paralysis (mummy fear aura). Fear is a mental effect, not poison.
- **Hold Person** does nothing to non-humanoid "monstrosities" (minotaurs).
- **Medusa petrification:** save vs Petrification, no ability-score modifier; looking away / at the feet lets you fight at **–4 to hit**; Stone to Flesh reverses it.
- **Trolls:** regenerate; must be **burned** (or acid) to stay dead. Rowan's flaming sword is especially effective (+2 hit, +1d4 fire).
- **Crystal ball** (Bruce): scry a room's contents — visual 3×/day, visual + audio 1×/day; higher success in familiar areas. Used to pre-scout rooms this session.
- **Rat-folk (Burt):** can identify a potion by scent before drinking. (Burt forgot and chugged an unknown potion → gaseous form.)
- **Potion of heroism:** drinker fights as one level higher (extra HP, saves, abilities) for the duration. Consumables like this "don't break the game" the way permanent magic items can — the DM's stated reason to be stingy with the latter.
- **Hell hounds** (this room): fire breath (save vs dragon breath); the encounter had a non-combat bypass (toss a coin into their hoard).
- **DM's forward warning:** the levels below get physically smaller, but the monsters "ramp up pretty ridiculously" from here down.
